Proving the concept of a luxury goods authentication service with prototype.

While the first negociation with Louis Vuitton for a MVP was ongoing, I planed several user flows and presented demos (interactives & videos). Being able to demonstrate the product’s main feature, without having write a line of code permitted us to win a first $350k deal.

View project ->

I started by mapping the different screens depending their contextual usage (either used by a transporter, a merchant, or the client). At this stage, wireframes were enough to highlight main user-flows and iterate quickly to fit business needs.

The wireframes have then been imported into slides to present different user journeys to the client and technical partners. Low-fidelity UI allowed anyone to keep focusing on the global user experience and flow of interactions rather than details on the interface.

I also developped short personas to humanize the entire supply-chain from raw material extraction to primary and secondary market. This step was necessary to bridge the gap between evolving states of an item anchored on the blockchain (to prove its authenticity) and allowing a coherent narrative delivered to the final user.

Once all wireframes and user-flows validated with key partners, I created on Figma + InVision screens with higher visual fidelity. Knowing that another aesthetic will be applied on the final version, the challenge at this stage was to validate the development of a first MVP, with the user experience of a finished product in mind.

I recorded interactions of the live prototype on Invision, and edited a 3 minutes video to show use cases in the real world.
