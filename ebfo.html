Creating series of reports covers for the European Commission.

ConsenSys has been selected by European Commission to educate Members of the European Parliament about the blockchain technology. This mission is held by the European Union Blockchain Observatory and Forum, which runs a serie of workshops, conferences and reports.

From the existing website developped by an external agency, I produced a consistent layout for 13 upcoming reports, regularly published during two years.

View project ->

For the first edition I worked more on the layout than the cover itself. Indeed, the layout would not change for the upcoming serie of reports, instead of their covers, treating different topics.

This project was a good occasion for me to use Processing: an environment of development based on Java to design with code.

A javascript version of Processing also exists, P5js, which might be handy for web projects.

I kept the gradient made of two shades of blue, as the constant graphical element , and play around representations of blocks and hash.

Unfortunately I couldn’t dedicate more time for these visuals, as I was on another client projects in the meantime. So I decided to jump on Figma, but keeping the logic of parametric design started on Processing (which was more “generative design” strictly speaking). This move permits me to produce more compositions at a glance, by shifting a few variables.

I exported in PDF format a multitude of covers, and accompanied my colleague to use inDesign, as much technically as conceptually to work on layout I made for him previously, and simple design guide lines I wanted to be respected visually.

My colleague didn’t use all covers I made, but two years later all reports are now online and accessible on eublockchainforum.eu/reports
